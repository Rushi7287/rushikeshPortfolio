Standard Objects

2.a Standard Objects

üé§INTERVIEW SECTION
30-Second Interview Answer

Standard Objects are predefined objects provided by Salesforce to manage common business processes

like Sales, Service, and Marketing. Examples include Account, Contact, Opportunity, Lead, and Case.

They come with built-in relationships, fields, and functionality that support CRM operations.

2-Minute Technical Explanation
  • Salesforce provides standard objects out of the box to support common CRM needs.
  • Some core standard objects:
  • Account ‚Üí Company or Organization
  • Contact ‚Üí Person associated with Account
  • Opportunity ‚Üí Sales deal
  • Lead ‚Üí Potential customer
  • Case ‚Üí Customer support ticket
  • User ‚Üí Salesforce user
  • Task/Event ‚Üí Activities
  • Standard objects:
  • Cannot be deleted
  • Have predefined standard fields
Support customization (add custom fields)
  • Have predefined relationships
  • They form the base of Salesforce data model.

Best Practices
  • Use standard objects when possible.
  • Avoid creating duplicate custom object if standard exists.
  • Extend using custom fields.
  • Use record types for different business processes.
  • Avoid over-customizing core objects.
When NOT to Use Standard Object
  • If business requirement:
  • Completely different lifecycle
  • No relation to CRM model
  • Custom logic heavy
  • Then create custom object.
